The Essence

My name is Anna McDermott. I am homegrown in the Pacific Northwest, though am an avid traveller with a goal to see as much of the world as possible. I am currently attending the University of Washington in Tacoma as a Master of Science candidate in geospatial technologies. I also enjoy spending time with friends and family, taking walks, and playing with my cats, Monroe and Lady Marilyn Grey.

My passion for wildlife and conservation has given me countless amazing experiences. I am lucky to have worked at a wildlife rehabilitation sanctuary, a zoo, and in the fields of South Africa and the Greater Yellowstone Ecosystem. The desire to contribute to conservation work led me to a GIS program that opened my eyes to a new and exciting path, sparking my enthusiasm to pursue further geospatial studies.

Another love of mine is music. I grew up singing and playing viola, and while in college, I started playing traditional Javanese gamelan. I had known about it my entire life as my dad was a music and composition professor, but was thrilled to finally play come college. It stole my heart just as it had my dad's (a music/composition professor) and I have continued to play throughout the years, including a time of study in the city of Solo, Java, Indonesia. While in Washington, I play with the Seattle group Gamelan Pacifica.



The Education

GIS Certificate from University of Washington in Tacoma, 2017.
